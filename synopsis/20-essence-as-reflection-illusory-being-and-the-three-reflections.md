# Essence as Reflection: Illusory Being and the Three Reflections

*The eleventh installment of Section III — and the first in the **Doctrine of Essence**, read closely. §19 brought Being to its own dissolution: its determinations proved to be **posited**, not immediate, and Being withdrew into its **ground** — into **Essence** (*Wesen*), being that *has been* (*gewesen*). This installment takes the first step inside. Essence is at first only the **negativity** that drove all of Being's passing-over, now taken for itself; and its first move is to set the whole realm of being over against itself as the **unessential** — a distinction that, made externally, at once collapses. What is left of being within essence is not a second world but a mere **illusory being** (*Schein*): an immediacy that *is* only as **sublated**, a being whose whole being is its own negatedness. Yet this shine is not a foreign sham smuggled in from outside; it is **essence's own shining within itself** — and that shining, the movement of the negative relating to nothing but itself, is **reflection** (*Reflexion*). Reflection unfolds in three moments: **positing** reflection, essence's self-movement that posits its own immediacy; **external** reflection, which presupposes a given immediate and relates to it as something *found* (the reflection of the understanding); and **determining** reflection, their unity, in which reflection posits its own presupposition and the determinacies it seemed to find prove to be its own — the threshold of the **determinations of reflection** (identity, difference, contradiction) that §21 will develop. Here the new logic shows its full face: not *passing-over* (*Übergehen*) but *shining-into* (*Scheinen*); not a sequence of immediacies but a self-relating movement that stays with itself in its other.*

---

## I. Where We Are: From the Threshold into Essence

The previous installment closed the Doctrine of Being by sublating it whole. Each determination of Being — quality, quantity, measure — had counted as something *immediately given*; pressed, each undid its own immediacy and disclosed that it was only **posited**, having being solely through its own negation. What did not vanish in all that passing-over was the **negativity** that drove it — the inner mediation always at work behind the surface of immediacy. **Essence** is that negativity taken now as a determination in its own right: being gone-into-itself, *being that has been* (§19).

That gives essence its peculiar starting position, and the whole difficulty of this sphere with it. Essence is not a *further* immediacy lying beyond being, a new and truer thing one reaches by leaving the world of being behind — that two-worlds picture §19 already forbade. Essence *is* the negation of immediacy; it has being only as the sublatedness of being. So essence cannot simply *be*, the way a quality or a quantum was; it can only **relate** — to the being it has negated, to itself as that negating. The categories of this sphere will therefore never stand alone as Being's did. Each is what it is only through its other, into which it **shines** rather than passes. The task of the present installment is to watch essence take its very first step on this ground: to see how the being it has negated returns as mere **shine**, and how the movement of that shine is the **reflection** that will generate everything to follow.

## II. The Essential and the Unessential — and Why the Distinction Fails

Essence's first gesture is the natural one, the one ordinary thought makes whenever it reaches for "the essential." Having negated being, essence sets itself over against it: essence is the true, the abiding, the **essential** (*das Wesentliche*); the immediate world of being, by contrast, is the **unessential** (*das Unwesentliche*) — the inessential surface, the mere outside, behind which the essence is supposed to lie. This is the standpoint of every search for a hidden core: strip away the inessential accidents, and the essence will be left.

But the distinction, made this way, does not hold, and its failure is instructive. To divide a field into the essential and the unessential, *something* must do the dividing — must mark *this* as core and *that* as husk. And that sorting is done from **outside**, by a third party doing the distinguishing; nothing in the material itself says which side is which. The "unessential" is unessential only *relative to* an essence picked out by someone's interest; shift the interest and the sorting shifts. So the essential/unessential distinction is **external and relative** — it falls outside the essence it claims to isolate, and therefore fails to be a distinction *of essence at all*. Essence, if it is to be the inner truth and not one more externally-imposed label, cannot stand merely *opposed* to the unessential as one side against another; for so opposed, it is itself only a relative term, just another being. The first move overshoots and collapses: what essence negates is not an unessential *other* standing beside it, but its own negated being — and that negated being, held within essence, is not the *unessential* but the **shine** (*Schein*).

## III. Illusory Being (*Schein*) — the Being That Remains Within Essence

Here is the chapter's pivotal and most easily misread category. When essence negates being, being is not simply annihilated; an immediacy *remains* — but an immediacy that now *is* only as negated, only as a moment held within essence. That remainder is **illusory being**, *Schein*. Its whole being consists in the **sublatedness of being**: it is, precisely, a non-being that yet shows; an immediacy that has no subsistence of its own but only the borrowed standing of something already negated. Hegel's formula is exact and worth slowing over: *Schein* is "reflected immediacy" — immediacy that is only *through* its own negation, so that, set against its mediation, it is nothing but the empty look of immediacy. It shines; it does not stand.

The word must be rescued from the very meaning it first suggests. *Schein* — "shine," "show," "semblance," "illusory being" — sounds like *sham*, mere subjective illusion, error to be cleared away; and philosophy has long handled it at arm's length. Ancient **skepticism** reduced the world to bare appearances and *suspended* assent about anything beyond them — keeping the shine, but as an immediate simply *given*, never as anything's own act. **Leibniz** graded sensory perception as *confused* against the distinct perception of the monad: such appearances are **well-founded** (*phaenomena bene fundata*), not false, but a lower clarity beneath which the truer is taken to lie. **Kant** found reason inevitably generating a transcendental *Schein* — an illusion *native* to reason, to be *exposed* and held in check though never made to vanish, and referred always to a thing-in-itself beyond reach. Hegel gathers these only to overturn what they share: in each the shine is still held *apart* from essence — taken as a bare given, or referred to a beyond — never as essence's own self-showing. He denies the apartness. The shine is not a deceptive skin over a hidden true thing; *there is no hidden thing behind it*. The immediacy that essence negated and yet retains is essence's *own* immediacy, the only immediacy it has. **The shine belongs to essence itself.**

## IV. The Shine Is Essence's Own — and Its Movement Is Reflection

Grant that the shine is essence's own, and the whole picture turns inside out. If *Schein* were the surface of some inner thing, then essence would be that thing and the shine its outer display — and we would be back in the two worlds. But essence is precisely *not* a thing; it is the negating, the sublatedness, the pure self-relating negativity. So the shine is not essence's *outside*; it is essence *showing within itself*. What shines, shines *in* essence and *as* essence — essence distinguishing itself from itself only to find, in the distinguished, nothing but itself.

That self-showing of the negative within itself has a name: **reflection** (*Reflexion*). The metaphor is optical and exact. Light *reflected* does not pass on into another medium and vanish, as Being's determinations passed over; it is turned back, bent into itself, returning to its source. So with essence: its movement is not transition into an other that it becomes and is lost in, but a **bending-back** in which it remains with itself throughout. Reflection is the *Schein* in motion — the shine grasped not as a static remainder but as the very activity of essence reflecting itself. And because it is a *movement*, it has moments; reflection is not one act but a structure of three, and getting that structure right is the whole technical labour of the chapter.

## V. Positing Reflection — Essence's Self-Movement

Take reflection first in its purest form, as essence's own self-movement with nothing presupposed. Essence is absolute negativity: the negative that negates *itself*. But a negation that negates itself does not thereby fall into blank nothing; in negating its own negating, it relates the negative to the negative and so returns — it is "the movement from nothing to nothing, and thereby back to itself." What this self-negating movement *produces* is an immediacy — but an immediacy that is sheerly **posited** (*gesetzt*), a being that is only its own non-being, a *Schein*. This is **positing reflection** (*setzende Reflexion*): essence positing the shine as its own, the negative bending back and, in the bending, giving itself the look of an immediate.

The decisive point — and the seed of everything that follows — is that the immediacy here is *not found but made*. There is no given starting point that reflection then works upon; the "immediate" is reflection's *own* deposit, a positedness through and through. Positing reflection is reflection that has, as yet, presupposed nothing: it is purely the self-moving negativity, and what looks in it like an immediate given is only the turning-back caught at the moment it appears to come to rest. Were this the whole of reflection, essence would be a closed self-relation with no purchase on anything. But the very immediacy it posits carries a second aspect, and that aspect opens reflection outward.

## VI. External Reflection, and Its Collapse into Determining Reflection

For the posited immediacy, once posited, *looks like a given* — it presents itself as something simply there, an immediate from which one might begin. Reflection taken at *this* face is **external reflection** (*äußere Reflexion*): reflection that **presupposes** an immediate, finds it already lying there, and relates to it from outside — comparing it, subsuming it under universals, drawing out its determinations as though they were waiting to be read off. This is the reflection of ordinary understanding, the everyday work of "reflecting on" a given: the datum is assumed, and thought plays over it externally, referring it to laws, genera, grounds it does not take to be its own doing. External reflection is indispensable and ubiquitous — it is what "reflection" usually means — and it is *one-sided*, because it forgets where its "given" came from.

And here the two collapse into one. External reflection presupposes an immediate as found; but that immediate, we have just seen, is nothing but what **positing reflection laid down**. The "given" external reflection begins from is its *own* presupposition — posited by reflection and then taken as if found. So external reflection, followed through, sublates its own externality: its presupposing *is* a positing, and the immediate it leans on is reflection's own product met coming back. The truth of the two together is **determining reflection** (*bestimmende Reflexion*): reflection that **posits its own presupposition** — in which the determinacy it finds is the very determinacy it has posited, and the determinacy it posits subsists, over against it, with the standing of something found. The product of determining reflection is therefore a peculiar hybrid: a **positedness that is at the same time a reflection-into-self**, a shine that has stiffened into a *fixed* determinacy holding firm on its own. Such a determinacy — a positedness that nonetheless stands like a being — is a **determination of reflection** (*Reflexionsbestimmung*). With it the chapter hands over to the next: identity, difference, and contradiction are exactly these — the determinations in which reflection's self-positing shine first congeals into the categories the old logic called the laws of thought.

## VII. Three Misreadings, Answered Directly

**The first is the sham-misreading**: that *Schein* means mere illusion — a subjective error, a false appearance to be cleared away so the truth behind it can be seen. This is the reading the word invites and the one the chapter is built to defeat. The shine is not the false *over against* a truth lying elsewhere; *there is no elsewhere*. It is essence's own immediacy, the only immediacy essence has. To treat *Schein* as removable illusion is precisely the **apartness** §III diagnosed — holding the shine off from essence as something merely given or referred elsewhere — and so keeping the two worlds the whole sphere exists to abolish.

**The second is the psychological misreading**: that "reflection" names an act of *ours* — a thinker turning thought back on a topic, a mental operation performed by a subject on an object. That is **external** reflection mistaken for the whole, and lifted out of the Logic into psychology besides. The reflection at issue here is not something a mind does to a given; it is the **objective self-movement of essence** — the negative relating to itself. Our subjective reflecting is a late and derivative case of it, not its meaning. Hegel is describing the structure of the thing, not the habits of the thinker.

**The third is the hidden-thing misreading**: that behind the shine sits an essence-substance, a true *Ding* of which the shine is the display — so that essence after all is a being, merely a deeper one. This collapses essence back into the very immediacy it is the negation of. Essence is **not** a thing that shines; it is the *shining itself*, the self-relating negativity. The category that will keep tempting us to re-install a hidden substrate — first as *thing*, later as *force*, later as *substance* — is exactly what the Doctrine of Essence will, step by step, dissolve.

## VIII. What the Method Did Here — Observations

The first observation is that **the new mode of the dialectic is now fully in force.** §19 announced the change — *passing-over* gives way to *shining-into* — but announced it from the far shore of Being. Here it is enacted. Nothing in this installment *passed over* into something else and vanished; the essential/unessential, the shine, the three reflections all **bent back** into essence, each the other's own moment. Reflection is that bending-back made the explicit law of movement. One can feel the difference in the very awkwardness of the prose: Being's categories could be lined up; Essence's must be said twice, each through its other, because neither *is* apart from the relating.

The second observation is that **a discredited category has been rehabilitated — and a method of rehabilitation shown.** *Schein* entered as the name for sham, the thing every prior philosophy wanted gone. The chapter does not defend illusion; it shows that what looked like mere illusion, followed honestly, is essence's own self-showing — that the negative, taken as *belonging* rather than as foreign, is productive. This is the same move that will, in §21, rescue **contradiction** from the disgrace the old logic consigned it to. The pattern is worth naming: the determinations the understanding throws away as defects are, read dialectically, the engine.

The third observation is that **the "laws of thought" have been given a derivation, not a list.** Identity, difference, contradiction were handed down by the old logic as fixed axioms, externally collected. We can now see where they come from: they are **determinations of reflection** — the points at which essence's self-positing shine stiffens into a fixed determinacy. That they are *posited* (made by reflection) and yet *subsist* (stand like beings) is the whole secret of their later instability. The next installment cashes this out.

## IX. What Comes Next

With reflection in hand, essence can begin to generate its determinate content:

- **§21 — the Determinations of Reflection**: **identity** (and why "A = A," taken alone, is empty and already harbours difference), **difference** (through *diversity* and *opposition*, driving into **contradiction**), and the rehabilitation of contradiction as the inner pulse of essence rather than a fault to be avoided — the determinations driving themselves toward their **ground**.
- **§22 — Ground**: contradiction "driving itself toward its ground"; ground and grounded; the splitting of ground into form, matter, and content; the accumulation of **conditions**; and the passage by which, when the conditions are complete, the grounded steps forth immediately as **Existence** — essence's first move out of pure inwardness toward **Appearance**.
- **Beyond**: **Appearance** (*Erscheinung*) — the thing and its properties, appearance and law, the essential relations — and then **Actuality** (*Wirklichkeit*) — substance, causality, reciprocity — where essence and appearance are fully united and the sphere passes, in its turn, into the **Concept**.

## Coda

Being had let us stand on a surface and watch each foothold give way to the next; Essence sends us beneath the surface, only to teach us that there is no second floor — that the "depth" is the surface bent back on itself, knowing itself as posited. We came looking for the essential behind the inessential and found that the division could not be drawn from outside; we came looking for the thing behind its shine and found that the shine was essence's own, and the looking-behind was essence reflecting itself. *What the Doctrine of Being met as immediacy, the Doctrine of Essence meets again as posited — and the whole art of this sphere is to hold what merely shines and what truly grounds in one thought, never letting the shine harden into a hidden thing, nor the ground evaporate into a mere show; for here, for the first time, nothing simply is — everything is only through its other, shining within it, and turning back.*
