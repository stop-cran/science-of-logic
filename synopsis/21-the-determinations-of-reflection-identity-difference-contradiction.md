# The Determinations of Reflection: Identity, Difference, Contradiction

*The twelfth installment of Section III — and the second in the **Doctrine of Essence**, read closely. §20 disclosed **reflection**: essence's shining within itself, the negative that stays with itself in its other, culminating in **determining reflection**, whose product is a **determination of reflection** (*Reflexionsbestimmung*) — a positedness that yet subsists like a being. This installment develops the three determinations — identity, difference, contradiction — behind the fixed "laws of thought" the old logic handed down and never derived. **Identity** (*A = A*) proves not the empty tautology it seems: its very form promises a difference it fails to deliver, and self-sameness shows itself to be the *active excluding* of difference — so identity already contains **difference** within it. Difference unfolds in turn: as **difference in itself** (self-related negativity, each of identity and difference containing the other); as **diversity** (*Verschiedenheit*), difference gone external, the mere "also" of things merely unlike, compared by an outside third into likeness and unlikeness; and as **opposition** (*Gegensatz*), the **positive** and the **negative**, each of which *is* only as the not-being of its other (the poles of a magnet, positive and negative charge, debit and credit). Pressed, opposition becomes **contradiction** (*Widerspruch*): each side, taken as self-subsistent, is what it is only by excluding the very other it requires to be itself — the bugbear the old logic proscribed, which Hegel reclaims as the **root of all movement and vitality**. Contradiction does not simply cancel; it **resolves** — the self-excluding sides *go to ground* (*zu Grunde gehen*), and the unity that holds them as sublated is **Ground** (*Grund*), the threshold of §22. Here the categorial-not-empirical guardrail holds: the Logic supplies the *form* of opposition, not the physics of the magnet.*

---

## I. Where We Are: From Reflection to Its Determinations

The previous installment won reflection as essence's mode of movement — not *passing-over* into an other and vanishing, as in Being, but *shining* within itself, bending back, staying with itself in its distinctions. And it ended at **determining reflection**: the reflection that posits its own presupposition, whose deposit is a peculiar hybrid — a **positedness that is at once a reflection-into-self**, a shine that has stiffened into a *fixed* determinacy holding firm on its own. Such a determinacy is a **determination of reflection** (*Reflexionsbestimmung*).

Now we must watch these determinations actually arise. The old logic bequeathed three **laws of thought** — the law of identity (*A = A*), the law of contradiction (*A is not not-A*), the law of the excluded middle (*either A or not-A, no third*) — and took them as fixed axioms, self-evident and externally collected, the bedrock of correct thinking, requiring and admitting no derivation. Hegel's wholly different claim is that such laws are the frozen deposit of three **determinations of reflection** — **identity**, **difference**, **contradiction** — moments through which essence distinguishes itself from itself, each *generated* by the movement of the last, each one-sided, each — taken as the whole truth — false. What the tradition laid side by side as a list, the *Logic* exhibits as a **movement**: identity giving way to difference, difference sharpening through diversity into opposition, opposition breaking into contradiction, and contradiction resolving into ground — the law of identity finding its place at identity, the "law of diversity" at diversity, and both non-contradiction and excluded middle answered within contradiction. The "laws of thought" are the stations of that movement, frozen and mislabelled as first principles.

## II. Identity

Essence, reflected wholly into itself, is at first **simple self-relation**: the pure identity of essence with itself, *A = A*. This looks like the emptiest and safest of all determinations — the very type of a truth beyond dispute. It is neither empty in the way it thinks nor safe.

Consider what the law of identity actually says. "*A* is *A*"; "a planet is a planet"; "spirit is spirit." The **form** of the proposition — subject, copula, predicate — *promises a difference*: to say "*A* is…" is to set out as though something will be predicated, some content unfolded. And then the sentence delivers nothing but *A* back again. The form holds out a distinction and the content retracts it; the proposition refutes, by its own shape, the bare self-sameness it asserts. Every attempt to *state* pure identity thus already performs the doubling — *A* as subject, *A* as predicate, the same held apart from itself in order to be equated with itself — that pure identity was supposed to exclude.

And that is the speculative point. Identity is not the abstract, inert sameness the understanding takes it for; it is **self-relation as the active excluding of difference**. To be identical-with-itself is to hold off, to negate, an otherness — which means the otherness is *there*, within the relation, as the very thing excluded. Pure light and pure darkness — as the logic of Being already remarked — are equally invisible; one sees only in their difference. So too identity without difference is indistinguishable from empty night. **Identity contains difference within itself** — not as a foreign intrusion but as its own inner moment, the negativity it *is*. With this, identity has, of itself, shown itself to be its opposite. We turn to difference.

## III. Difference in Itself — the Absolute Difference

Take difference first in its purest form, as it issues immediately from identity: **difference in itself**, *absolute difference*. Not yet the difference *between* two given things — that comes later — but difference as such, the simple negativity that reflection is. Just as identity showed itself to contain difference, so this difference contains identity: it is *self-related* difference, difference that refers only to itself, and in referring only to itself it is *identical with itself* as difference. Each of the two — identity and difference — thus proves to be **the whole**: identity is the identity of itself and difference, and difference is the difference of itself and identity. Neither is a fixed determinacy standing apart; each is itself only through the other, and contains the other as its own moment.

This is the abstract core of the matter, and it is easily lost, so let it be put flatly: *identity and difference are not two independent determinacies that happen to be compared, but two names for one self-relating negativity, each of which, pressed, becomes the other.* Difference held purely in itself, however, cannot remain in this equilibrium. Precisely because it is the difference that is *identical with itself*, it lets its two sides — identity and difference — fall apart as **self-subsistent**, each indifferent to the other. Difference-in-itself thereby collapses into its next, degraded form: **diversity**.

## IV. Diversity — Difference Gone External

In **diversity** (*Verschiedenheit*), the two moments of difference fall asunder into mutual **indifference**. Things are now merely *diverse*: each is self-identical, complete in itself, and its difference from the others is a matter of external comparison — they differ "also," alongside being what they each are. The difference has fallen *outside* the things and into a **comparing third** — into the observer, the understanding, who holds two items together and pronounces them **alike** (*gleich*) in one respect, **unlike** (*ungleich*) in another.

Likeness and unlikeness are thus the form difference takes when it has become external: no longer the thing's own self-relating negativity, but a relation instituted by comparison and split across "respects" — *A* is like *B* in colour, unlike in weight. And the comparing understanding keeps the two apart, in different regards, so that likeness and unlikeness never meet. But this separation cannot hold, for it is the *same* comparison that pronounces both, the *same* two items that are said to be alike and unlike; likeness and unlikeness themselves prove to be inseparable, each intelligible only against the other. The much-repeated "law" — that all things are **diverse**, that no two are perfectly alike — reaches its limit and its truth in **Leibniz's** principle of the **identity of indiscernibles** (*no two leaves of the garden exactly the same*): pushed to consistency, mere external diversity points beyond itself to a difference that is *internal* to the thing, a difference the thing makes to itself. External diversity thereby sublates itself into a difference that is *determinate* and *self-related* — into **opposition**.

## V. Opposition — the Positive and the Negative

**Opposition** (*Gegensatz*) is the unity — the truth — of identity and difference. Here difference is at last **determinate**: not the empty "not the same" of diversity, but a difference in which each side is what it is *precisely and only* through its relation to its other. The two sides are the **positive** and the **negative**. And the deep point, the one the understanding cannot hold, is this: each of them is *self-subsistent* — each claims to be a whole standing on its own, "positive in itself" or "negative in itself" — and yet each *is* what it is only as the **not-being of the other**. The positive is the self-identical that is *not* the negative; the negative is the self-identical that is *not* the positive; strip away the relation to the other and neither has any determinacy left at all.

The world of opposition is full of instances, and they are exact, not decorative. The **north and south poles of a magnet** give the opposition at its starkest: neither pole is anything except as the opposite of the other — cut the magnet and you do not obtain a lone north, but two fresh magnets, each again two-poled. Others show the same *sign*-opposition, where each side is determinate only as the contrary of the other even where the two can be held apart: **positive and negative charge**; **debit and credit**, where every credit entered in one account is answered by a debit in another and the books close only because the two are one relation read twice; the **creditor and the debtor**; and, in bare arithmetic, the **positive and negative magnitudes**, where *+a* and *−a* are opposite only relative to a chosen direction, and either may be taken as the positive. In each, the positive is not first positive and then, additionally, opposed; its *whole content* is to be the negation of its negative. (The guardrail of §17–§20 holds here too: the Logic does not deduce magnetism or the sign conventions of algebra. It exhibits the categorial **form** — determinate opposition — of which these are the instances. The physics is found; the category is thought.)

Now press the two sides. Each was to be *self-subsistent* — a positive-in-itself, a negative-in-itself, each excluding the other and complete without it. Yet each *is* only as the exclusion of the other, which is to say only *through* the other it excludes. Each therefore contains, at its own core, the very other it was defined against — and cannot both contain it and exclude it. Opposition, taken seriously, has become **contradiction**.

## VI. Contradiction — and Its Resolution into Ground

Here is the pivot of the whole sphere, and the determination the old logic most feared. In **contradiction** (*Widerspruch*), each of the opposed sides is **the whole**: the positive is the entire opposition (itself *plus* its excluded negative held within it), and so is the negative. Each is self-subsistent, and each is, in the same respect and at the same time, the *sublation* of the other and therefore of itself. Each excludes the other and thereby excludes itself; each is only by being the perishing of what it needs in order to be. This is not a mistake in reasoning to be tidied away. It is the determinate structure of the opposed determinations themselves.

The tradition met this with the **law of contradiction** — nothing can both be and not be, *A* cannot be not-*A* — and the **law of the excluded middle** — everything is either *A* or not-*A*, with no third. Hegel's response is not to wave logic away but to locate what these laws *miss*. The excluded middle is answered in two steps. First: there *is* a third — the very thing the "middle" excludes is *A* itself, the something that is neither merely *+A* nor merely *−A* but the indifferent substrate on which both are posited. And that substrate, pressed, proves to be the **ground** in which *+A* and *−A* are held as one. The horror of contradiction is, in turn, misplaced, because **contradiction is the root of all movement and vitality**: "something moves," in Hegel's famous formula, "not because at one *now* it is here and at another *now* it is there, but because in one and the same *now* it is here and not here" — the paradoxes of motion the ancient dialecticians pressed, Zeno's arrow foremost, resolved not by denying the contradiction but by recognizing motion *as* the existing contradiction. Everything finite, everything living, everything that changes, harbours contradiction within it; that is why it does not rest. **A thing is alive only insofar as it contains contradiction, and is the power to hold and endure it.**

But contradiction is not the last word — it is precisely what *cannot* be a last word, and its unrest is the proof. The opposed sides, each excluding itself in excluding the other, **cannot subsist**; they collapse. Yet what they collapse *into* is not blank nothing. In perishing, each side negates itself, and the negation of the self-excluding opposition is the **unity** that was, all along, sustaining it — the affirmative result in which the opposed determinations are held as *sublated* moments. The German says it in one phrase Hegel cannot resist: the contradictory sides **go to ground** — *zu Grunde gehen*, at once "to perish" and "to go to the **ground**." Contradiction resolved is **Ground** (*Grund*): the deeper unity from which the opposed determinations issue and which holds them together. With this the determinations of reflection have completed themselves — identity into difference, difference into opposition, opposition into contradiction, contradiction into ground — and essence has its first form of genuine **mediation**.

## VII. Three Misreadings, Answered Directly

**The first is the incoherence-misreading**: that Hegel "denies the law of non-contradiction," licenses asserting *P* and *not-P* together, and so forfeits the right to reason at all. This mistakes dialectical contradiction for the formal contradiction of a careless proof. Hegel is not affirming a flat conjunction *P ∧ ¬P* and resting in it; he is describing an *unstable* structure — determinations that exclude each other yet require each other — whose very instability **drives** it beyond itself into ground. Formal non-contradiction governs fixed determinacies held apart by the understanding, and within that domain it stands. The dialectical claim is about what happens when such determinacies are thought *through*: they do not sit still to be conjoined; they move. Contradiction here is not a proposition to be believed but a *process* to be followed to its resolution.

**The second is the empty-tautology misreading**, in two opposite forms. One says: identity is *A = A*, a barren tautology, so the "laws of thought" are trivial and thought must look elsewhere for content. The other says the reverse: that *A = A*, abstract self-sameness, is the supreme and sufficient law of all correct thinking. Both freeze identity as the understanding's inert sameness. The chapter's finding is that identity, taken strictly, is *neither* trivial *nor* sufficient: its own form already performs a difference, and pursued honestly it *yields* difference, opposition, and contradiction. Identity is a real moment — but a moment, whose truth is the concrete whole that includes its other.

**The third is the everything-is-relative misreading**: that if all determinations contradict themselves and pass into their opposites, then nothing is fixed, any claim is as good as its negation, and dialectic is a license for slippage. This inverts the result. Contradiction does not dissolve into an indifferent flux where "anything goes"; it resolves **determinately** — *this* opposition goes to *this* ground. The movement is not the loss of all footing but the disclosure of a *deeper* footing, the ground that the fixed determinations presupposed. Dialectic ends one-sided fixities not to abolish truth but to drive it inward, from the immediate to the grounded.

## VIII. What the Method Did Here — Observations

The first observation is that **the "laws of thought" have been derived rather than assumed.** What the old logic posited as self-evident axioms — identity, non-contradiction, excluded middle — the *Logic* has located and derived from the **determinations of reflection** — identity, difference, contradiction — each generated from the last, each true only as a one-sided moment of a movement that does not stop at any of them. This is the promissory note of §20 (VIII) redeemed: the categories the tradition *collected* are now *deduced*, and their notorious insufficiency — that no actual thought ever rests in bare *A = A* — is explained by their place in the sequence rather than papered over.

The second observation is that **contradiction has been rehabilitated as the engine, not the enemy, of thought.** §20 rescued *Schein* from the charge of mere illusion; here the same move rescues *Widerspruch* from the charge of mere error. The understanding treats contradiction as the one thing to be avoided at all costs; the *Logic* shows it to be the one thing that *moves* — the inner unrest without which nothing would pass beyond itself. The pattern, now twice enacted, is the signature of the whole sphere: the determination the understanding throws away as a defect is, read speculatively, the source of life.

The third observation is that **each determination of reflection is double, and that is why it drives.** Every one of them — identity, diversity, positive, negative — is at once a **positedness** (made by reflection, existing only in its relation to its other) and a **reflection-into-self** (standing, claiming to be self-subsistent). This is exactly the hybrid §20 identified in determining reflection. Because each is *both*, none can rest: what is only-through-its-other cannot also be self-standing, and the friction of that doubleness is what carries identity to difference, difference to opposition, opposition to contradiction, and contradiction to ground. The instability is not incidental; it is built into the form of a reflected determinacy as such.

## IX. What Comes Next

With the determinations of reflection resolved into their unity, essence has reached its first genuine mediation, and the sphere can develop it:

- **§22 — Ground**: **ground and grounded**, and why each requires the other; the splitting of ground into **form** and **matter**, form and **content**; the **formal**, **real**, and **complete** grounds, and how ground-relation slides toward triviality (the "dormitive virtue" that grounds sleep by a power-to-sleep); the accumulation of **conditions**; and the passage by which, when the conditions are complete, the grounded steps forth immediately as **Existence** (*Existenz*) — essence's first move out of pure inwardness toward **Appearance**.
- **Beyond**: **Appearance** (*Erscheinung*) — the thing and its properties, the world of appearance, appearance and law, the essential relations (whole and parts, force and its expression, inner and outer) — and then **Actuality** (*Wirklichkeit*) — substance, causality, reciprocity — where essence and appearance are fully united and the sphere passes, in its turn, into the **Concept**.

## Coda

The old logic wrote its first principles on the wall — *A is A*; nothing both is and is not; either this or not-this — and took them for the unshakable floor of all thinking. We have watched that floor prove to be a stairway. Identity, questioned, could not keep difference out; difference, pursued, sharpened into opposition; opposition, taken at its word, broke into contradiction; and contradiction, far from halting thought, turned out to be the very thing that moves it — and, in moving, to point beneath itself to the ground it had always presupposed. *What the understanding forbids as the death of thought is, for reason, its life; the determinations by which we hold the world apart are the determinations by which the world holds together; and every fixed opposition, followed without fear to its end, does not leave us with rubble but hands us down — zu Grunde — to the ground on which it stood.*
