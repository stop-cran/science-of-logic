# Existence and the Thing: The In-Itself, the Play of Matters, and the Passage into Appearance

*The fourteenth installment of Section III — the fourth in the **Doctrine of Essence**, and the first of its second section, **Appearance** (*Erscheinung*). §22 ended with the totality of conditions complete, the **fact itself** (*die Sache selbst*) stepping forth, and the **ground sinking away** in the act of grounding — so that what stands before us is a **mediated immediacy**. Taken for itself, that immediacy is **Existence** (*Existenz*), the being that has **come out of ground** — *ex-sistere*, to step forth — no longer the bare, groundless immediacy of **Being** nor the qualitative **determinate being** (*Dasein*) of the first sphere, but an immediacy that **carries its mediation within it as sublated**. Existence is at once a **world of existences** reciprocally conditioning one another; and the existent, taken as **reflected into itself**, is the **Thing** (*Ding*). The Thing splits into the **thing-in-itself** (*Ding-an-sich*) and its outer existence — and here Hegel settles accounts with **Kant**: the thing-in-itself is **not** a rich unknowable **beyond** but the wholly **abstract**, empty **in-itself**, the thing with every relation stripped away; its vaunted **unknowability** is a **tautology**, self-inflicted by the abstraction that produced it — nothing is hidden behind the appearance because the "behind" is the **poorest** of categories. Determinateness returns as **property** (*Eigenschaft*) — the determinate way the thing **relates to others while remaining its own** (*eigen*), the in-itself made explicit — and the Thing proves to **be** its properties, with no bare **substrate** propping them up. The properties, made self-subsistent, become **free matters** (*freie Materien*) — the free-matters physics and chemistry of his day (**heat-matter** or caloric, **light-**, **electrical** and **magnetic matter**, the chemists' **coloring** and **odor matters**) — and the Thing dwindles to a mere **"also,"** a **porous** bringing-together held only by the **"this,"** the **One and the Many** of §14 returning at a reflected level (recollection, not analogy). The Thing then **dissolves**: negative unity and indifferent matters each collapse into the other, the immediacy of Existence shows itself as **posited**, as a **shining** — and Existence completes itself into **Appearance**: **essence must appear**. Three misreadings answered (the thing-in-itself as unknowable **noumenon**; **Existence** confused with existentialist facticity or the logician's quantifier; the Thing read as a literal **bundle of matters**), and the **categorial-not-empirical** guardrail held: the Logic supplies the *form* of thinghood, not the physics of any given thing.*

---

## I. Where We Are: From Ground to Existence

The previous installment watched grounding complete itself and, in completing itself, disappear. The **formal** ground merely renamed its effect; the **real** ground adduced a different content but left the connection arbitrary; only the **complete** ground grounded the connection — and it did so by passing into **condition**. The scattered conditions accumulated; and when the ground and the **whole set of conditions** stood together — the **absolutely unconditioned**, the **fact itself** — the thing **stepped forth of itself**, at which very moment the ground, its work done, **sank away**. What was left was not a grounded thing still clinging to its ground, but a thing that simply *is*: a **mediated immediacy**, an immediacy with its mediation folded inside it as sublated.

That result is the beginning of a new category. Essence has, for the first time, **stepped out of pure inwardness**. In its first section it was reflection turned upon itself — illusory being, the determinations of reflection, ground — a hidden self-relating that never quite reached a *there*. Now it has issued into one. The immediacy that has come forth from ground, taken **for itself**, is **Existence** (*Existenz*); and with it the *Logic* opens the second section of the Doctrine of Essence, **Appearance** (*Erscheinung*), whose first chapter is **Existence** and its **Thing**. Where the first section asked what essence *is* behind what shows, this section watches essence *come to show* — and finds that it has nothing behind the showing to withhold.

## II. Existence — the Immediacy Sprung from Ground (*ex-sistere*)

The word must be heard exactly. **Existence** translates *Existenz*, and Hegel leans on the Latin *ex-sistere*, **to step forth**, **to stand out from**. Existence is not being at large; it is the being **that has come out of a ground**. This is why it must be kept apart from the two immediacies already behind us. It is not **Being** (*Sein*), the utterly groundless, presuppositionless immediacy with which the science began — the poorest *there is*, without so much as a mediation to sublate. And it is not **determinate being** (*Dasein*), the qualitative being-there of the first sphere, whose determinacy was a simple **quality** immediately its own. Existence is an immediacy that has a **behind** — a ground — and has *taken that behind up into itself*. It is **reflected immediacy**: being as the truth of a mediation that has consummated and cancelled itself.

Because every existent is what it is only as the outcome of a set of conditions, and each condition is itself an existent conditioned by others, Existence is never a single thing. It is at once a **world of existences**, an endless web in which each stands forth only through the others, and grounds others in turn — the mesh of ground-and-condition from §22 now **congealed** into a standing field of mutually supporting things. This is the first sense in which essence has become **outward**: it is dispersed into a manifold of immediate existents, each pointing beyond itself to the rest.

But an existent can be **taken in two ways**. As the sheer immediate result — the *there* it has become — it is mere existence, one node in the web. As **reflected into itself** — as the *essential*, the identity that the whole play of its conditions has deposited and that now stands on its own — it is a **Thing** (*Ding*). The Thing is Existence grasped as **self-subsistent**, as the essential existent that *has* an existence rather than merely *being* one. With the Thing, the manifold of Existence acquires centers; and the analysis of Existence becomes the analysis of the Thing.

## III. The Thing-in-Itself — Hegel's Reckoning with Kant

The Thing at once divides. On one side it is the **thing-in-itself** (*Ding-an-sich*) — what the thing *is* apart from its relations, its bare inward identity. On the other it is the thing's **existence**, its being-there for and among others. And no term of the *Logic* has been more **misheard by the tradition** than this one, so the chapter's chief labor is to say what the in-itself actually is.

The picture Hegel takes as his target — the **popular reception** of **Kant** more than Kant's own guarded doctrine — is of the thing-in-itself as a **hidden reality** lying **beyond** experience, of which appearances are the mere surface, forever screened from knowing. (Kant's own doctrine is more austere than this popular gloss: the *Ding an sich* is not a positively known plenitude but a bare *that-it-is*; and the **noumenon** through which it is thought is, in its **negative** sense, only a **limiting concept** [*Grenzbegriff*] that curbs the claims of sensibility — its **positive** sense, an object of intellectual intuition, being one we do not possess.) Hegel's stroke tells against **both**, and cuts deepest against the careful version: he **inverts** the picture without denying the term. The in-itself, he insists, is what remains of the thing **when every relation, every determinacy, every being-for-other has been abstracted away**. It is the product of a subtraction. And a subtraction that removes *all* determination leaves not a hidden plenitude but the **emptiest** of abstractions — the pure, indeterminate **in-itself as such**, the same on every side, indistinguishable from thing to thing. There is nothing **in** the thing-in-itself to know, not because a wall bars the way, but because the very operation that manufactured it stripped it of all content.

Hence the celebrated **unknowability** is a **tautology**, and a self-inflicted one. To form the concept of the thing-in-itself is to abstract from everything through which a thing could be known; to then announce that it cannot be known is merely to **repeat the abstraction as a verdict**. Knowing has not *failed* to reach the in-itself; there is simply nothing there for it to reach. Far from marking the humility of reason before an infinite depth, the Kantian "we cannot know things in themselves" records only that we have defined the in-itself as **that from which all knowability has been withdrawn**. The supposed **beyond** is the **poorest** category the Doctrine of Essence contains.

And it does not stay still. The thing-in-itself, being wholly indeterminate, has all its determinateness **outside** it, in the **external reflection** that stands over against it and relates it to other things. But that reflection is not really external: an in-itself whose entire determinacy lies in its relation-to-other is an in-itself that **has** its relation-to-other as its **own** truth. The in-itself must **take its outer determinateness back into itself** — and in doing so it ceases to be the empty *an-sich* and becomes a thing with **determinate character of its own**. That character is **property**.

## IV. Property — the Thing's Own, and the Reciprocal Action of Things

**Property** (*Eigenschaft*) is the determinateness of the Thing grasped no longer as a quality it merely *has* but as the way it **relates itself to other things while remaining itself**. The German makes the point the English half-hides: *Eigenschaft* is built on *eigen*, **own**. A property is a thing's **own**, the determinate mode in which it **holds itself out** into relation and **maintains** itself there. This lifts property beyond the earlier **quality** of the sphere of Being and beyond mere **constitution** (*Beschaffenheit*): where quality was simple and immediate, and constitution was the determinacy a thing takes on in its commerce with others *without* thereby being fixed as its own, property is that outward determinacy grown **self-preserving** — **constitution that has become determination** (*Bestimmung*), the in-itself of the thing **realized, and maintained, in its bearing toward others**. A thing "has" a property in the emphatic sense that in exercising it upon another the thing **shows what it is** and yet **stays its own**.

From this two things follow at once. First, the Thing **is nothing over and above its properties**. The old image of a bare **substrate** — a featureless *pin-cushion* or naked **bearer** into which properties are stuck, itself none of them — collapses, exactly as §22's critique of the empty substrate would lead us to expect. Strip a thing of every property and you are left not with the thing but with the empty thing-in-itself already convicted of vacancy. The Thing *is* the **determinate unity of its properties**; there is no further, hidden thing behind them clutching them together.

Second, since a property *is* only in the thing's **bearing toward others** — acid is what it is in dissolving the metal, the magnet in turning the needle — the Thing exists only in the **reciprocal action of things** (*Wechselwirkung der Dinge*). The thing-in-itself passes over into **being-for-other**; its properties are precisely the joints at which one thing meets another. And because a thing has **many** properties, each answering to a different other, the Thing presents itself as an **"also"** — a coexistence of determinacies, this property *and* that one *and* another, held together in one existent without any of them touching the rest. The **"also"** is the seam at which the Thing will begin to come apart.

## V. The Thing as a Play of Free Matters

Press on the **"also"** and the properties detach. If each property is a self-standing determinacy through which the thing acts, and the Thing is merely the place where they coincide, then the properties can be thought as **self-subsistent** in their own right — as **free matters** (*freie Materien*, *Stoffe*) of which the thing is composed. The property **"heat"** becomes **heat-matter** (caloric); light becomes **light-matter**; and alongside them stand **electrical** and **magnetic matter** — the *imponderable* fluids of the physics of Hegel's day — while chemistry reifies the sensible qualities into **coloring** and **odor matters** (*Farbstoff*, *Riechstoff*). The thing is resolved into an assemblage of these. This is not Hegel's invention: he takes over the **free-matters** doctrine of contemporary physics and chemistry and seizes on it as the exact categorial figure the Thing here assumes — the property **hypostatized into a stuff**.

Once the properties are free matters, the Thing is no longer their **unity** but their mere **collection**. It is an **"also"** through and through — an external **bringing-together** of independent matters, each of which could as well belong to another thing. What holds them in *this* thing is no longer an inner essence but only the bare **"this,"** the **One** that gathers a **Many** — precisely the figure of §14, the **One and the Many**, returning now at the reflected level of essence. And the device by which the physics let its matters coexist is instructive: **porosity**. Each matter is porous, riddled with pores that the other matters fill, so that all of them can occupy "the same" thing without merging — a **quantitative interpenetration** in which the Thing has become a pure **how-much** and **where**, a combination without a governing quality. The Thing has passed from **essential unity** to **external, quantitative composition**: a heap of matters lodged in one another's pores, held by nothing but the **"this."**

We should mark that this recurrence of the **One and the Many** is not a resemblance noticed from outside — the caution §22 insisted on. It is the same negativity of the **self-related One**, first won in the sphere of Being, now **recollected** in the richer element of essence, where the "ones" are self-subsistent *matters* and their unity a merely **posited** gathering. The category has not been reused by analogy; it has been **reached again**, deeper.

## VI. The Dissolution of the Thing and the Passage into Appearance

The Thing so conceived is a **contradiction**, and it dissolves under its own terms. On one side it is supposed to be the **negative unity**, the "this" that gathers the matters and makes them *one* thing; on the other it has surrendered all subsistence to the matters, which are **self-subsistent** and **indifferent** to being gathered here rather than elsewhere. So the Thing is at once the essential unity of the matters and **nothing but** their inessential collection. Each side undoes the other. If the matters are genuinely free, the Thing is a mere name for their contingent co-location and has no unity of its own; if the Thing is a genuine unity, the matters are **not** free but only its **moments**, and the whole edifice of self-subsistent stuffs falls.

Porosity makes the collapse explicit. To let the many matters occupy one thing at once, the physics must riddle each with **pores** that the others fill — but then each matter subsists only where the others do *not*, so that its advertised self-subsistence is at every point the **negation** of the rest, and the "independent" stuffs interpenetrate in the very place they are supposed to exclude one another. Their subsistence is thus at once asserted and withdrawn. And the matters need the **"this"** to be gathered into a thing at all, while the **"this"** needs the matters for any **content**; each is the mere positedness of the other. The self-subsistence advertised on both sides is **borrowed** on both sides, and therefore nowhere.

What this dissolution yields is not simply the disappearance of the Thing but a **change in the standing of its immediacy**. Existence entered as a **mediated immediacy** that seemed, once the ground had sunk away, to stand on its own. The Thing's collapse shows that this immediacy was, all along, a **posited** one — an immediacy that **is** only as the showing-forth of an essence, a **shining** (*Scheinen*) and no longer a self-standing *there*. The **essential** and the **unessential**, the thing and its existence, have run together: no **separate abstract substrate** stands *behind* the existent withholding itself — the existent *is* essence's **showing-forth**. That is the threshold of **Appearance** (*Erscheinung*), where essence exists *as* its own appearing. (The full identity of essence and appearance is not yet won: appearance will still set a **world-in-itself** against the **world of appearance**, and the essential relations still lie ahead; their complete unity is reserved for **Actuality**.) Existence, thought through to the end, has completed itself into appearance, and the sphere announces its governing law: **essence must appear**. Immediacy is now **posited as positedness** — the exact threshold at which the next installment will take up the **law of appearance**, the **world of appearance**, and the **essential relations**.

## VII. Three Misreadings, Answered Directly

**The first is the unknowable-noumenon misreading**: that Hegel's **thing-in-itself** is, after all, the popular Kantian **thing behind the appearance** — a real but inaccessible core — and that the chapter is a variation on transcendental modesty. It is the reverse. Hegel does not enrich the in-itself and lament our exclusion from it; he **empties** it and exposes the lament as circular. The in-itself is the thing with *all* determinacy abstracted away, and its unknowability is nothing but that abstraction **stated as a result**. The lesson is not that reason is barred from a hidden depth but that the "hidden depth," so defined, is the most **contentless** thought available — and that the truth of the thing lies wholly in its **properties**, its **relations**, its **appearing**, with nothing withheld behind them. What the critical tradition took for humility Hegel exhibits as a **tautology**.

**The second is the existentialist misreading**: that **Existence** here means what a later vocabulary means by it — brute **facticity**, the sheer *that-it-is* of the human being's contingent situation, so that "existence precedes essence." Hegel's *Existenz* is a **determinate category of essence**, not a mood or a metaphysics of the human situation. It names a **specific structure** — the **mediated immediacy** that has issued from ground and carries its mediation sublated within it — and it stands in an ordered sequence *between* ground and appearance, neither the groundless *Sein* with which the science began nor the existentialist's contingent facticity. Nor is it the **logician's "existence"** in any of its modern guises — the quantifier's *there is*, **Kant's** thesis that being "is not a real predicate," or **Frege's** existence-as-instantiation — for these concern *whether* something is instanced, whereas Hegel is tracing the **inner articulation** of essence's having stepped forth. (Where Hegel *does* join Kant on this ground — the ontological-proof dispute over whether existence can be "added" to a concept — his answer is the speculative one, not the quantifier's: essence does not **have** existence as a predicate; it **passes over into** existence.) The word is shared; the concept is not.

**The third is the bundle-of-matters misreading**: that Hegel is doing **physics** — endorsing caloric and coloring matters — or else a reductive **metaphysics** on which a thing just *is* a bundle of properties or stuffs (the **bundle picture** later associated with **Hume**, whose bundle was in fact of the *self*, and revived in the twentieth century). The **free matters** are neither an empirical thesis nor an ontological creed but the **categorial figure** the Thing necessarily assumes when its properties are made self-subsistent — the very figure the chapter goes on to **dissolve**. The passage takes over the free-matters physics precisely to show it collapsing into the **"also"** and its self-cancelling **porosity**, and it exhibits the bundle only to show that a bundle with **no** unity is not a thing at all, while a bundle *with* a unity has **not** dispensed with essence. The passage **diagnoses** reductive thinghood; it does not **assert** it — the Logic supplies the *form* of the Thing's dissolution, not a chemistry of stuffs.

## VIII. What the Method Did Here — Observations

The first observation is that **the critique of Kant is immanent, not external.** Hegel does not confront the thing-in-itself with a rival epistemology or claim some faculty Kant denied us. He **takes the category at its word** — grants that the in-itself is what is left when all relation is abstracted — and lets it convict itself: an in-itself so defined is empty, and its unknowability follows **analytically** from its manufacture. This is the same maneuver §21 ran on the laws of thought and §22 on sufficient reason: a fixed axiom of the tradition is **derived** and, in the deriving, **limited from within**. The **limit of knowledge** dissolves not because knowing is fortified but because the object of the alleged limit turns out to be the **poverty of a concept**.

The second observation is that **the essence-sphere re-runs the being-sphere's figures at a reflected level — and that this is recollection, not repetition.** The **One and the Many** of §14 return as the **"this"** and its **free matters**; the empty **substrate** disowned in §22 returns as the bare bearer disowned here; the **quality** and the **constitution** of the first sphere return transfigured as **property**. The governing recurrence is the largest: as *Dasein* threw up **Something** (*Etwas*, §11) — the first *self-related* determinate being — so **Existence** throws up the **Thing** (*Ding*), Existence reflected into itself as **self-subsistent**. The Thing is the **"Something" of the essence-sphere**, but reflected where *Etwas* was merely qualitative: not a determinate that simply *is* what it is, but one that *has* an existence, carrying its in-itself within it — which is why its very self-subsistence **divides at once** into in-itself and being-for-other, a division *Etwas* reached too (§12) but had to *win* progressively, through its reference to an *other*, whereas the Thing carries it reflected from the start. Each is the **same** determination **recognized again in a richer element**, exactly as the tightening of §22 required us to say. The Doctrine of Essence is not laying new bricks beside the old; it is **cognizing what Being only was**, and the recurrences are the visible track of that deepening. This is why "essence must appear" could be **earned** here rather than announced: the sphere had to run its inherited figures down to the point where their immediacy showed itself as **posited**.

The third observation is that **mediated immediacy has come of age.** §22 gave us the *event* of it — the ground sinking away so that a thing might stand forth. §23 gives us its *truth*: that the immediacy so won is a **posited** immediacy, an existence whose very standing-forth is a **shining** of essence. The disappearing-ground rhythm did not deposit a self-sufficient *there* after all; it deposited a **world that appears** — things that are wholly their properties, wholly their relations, wholly their manifesting, with no hidden core held back. The category has stopped **pointing beyond itself** to a hidden ground, only to discover that in ceasing to point beyond it has become **through and through a pointing-forth** — the appearing of an essence that has nothing left to hide.

## IX. What Comes Next

With Existence dissolved into appearing and the law **essence must appear** in force, the sphere can develop what showing-forth involves:

- **§24 — Appearance and the Essential Relation**: **Appearance** (*Erscheinung*) proper — the **law of appearance** (the stable, essential content that persists through the flux of the appearing), the **world of appearance** set against the **world-in-itself**, and the collapse of that opposition; then the **essential relations** (*wesentliche Verhältnisse*) — **whole and parts**, **force** (*Kraft*) **and its expression** (*Äußerung*), **inner and outer** — in which the two worlds are drawn into a single reciprocal structure.
- **Beyond**: **Actuality** (*Wirklichkeit*) — the unity of essence and appearance come to rest — with its **modal** categories (possibility, contingency, necessity), then **substance** and, at last, **causality** and **reciprocity**, where the transitive **efficacy** that ground could only prefigure finally arrives — and the Doctrine of Essence passes over into the **Concept**.

## Coda

The tradition's most solemn confession was that we cannot know the thing in itself — that behind every appearance stands a reality forever screened from thought, before which reason must fall silent. We have watched the confession turn out to be a **tautology**. The thing-in-itself was made by **stripping the thing of everything** through which a thing is known; to then call it unknowable is only to **say the stripping over again**. Nothing is withheld behind the appearance, because the "behind" is the emptiest thought in the whole Doctrine of Essence — and the truth of the thing was in front of us all along, in its **properties**, its **matters**, its **bearing** toward other things, its **appearing**. *The thing keeps no secret from us; it is nothing but its coming-forth. What looked like an infinite depth withheld from knowing was only the shadow cast by an abstraction — and once the abstraction lifts, essence has nowhere left to hide and no choice left but to appear.*
