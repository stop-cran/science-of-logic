# Being-for-self, the One, and the Many

*The fifth installment of Section III — a close reading of the determination in which the true infinite first comes to rest: **being-for-self** (*Fürsichsein*), being that has taken its otherness wholly inside itself and relates only to itself; its moment of **being-for-one** (*Sein-für-Eines*), in which the Other survives only as something ideal; the collapse of being-for-self into the **One** (*das Eins*); the **One and the Void**, and the logical truth of atomism; the dialectic of **Repulsion** and **Attraction** by which the One posits the Many and the Many collapse toward the One; and the self-sublation of the qualitative One that carries the whole sphere of Quality over into **Quantity**.*

---

## I. Where We Are: from the True Infinite to Being-for-self

The previous installment closed with the **true infinite** — the unity of finite and infinite, the self-returning whole that has its otherness inside itself rather than over against it as a limit. We named its result but did not develop it: being that no longer points beyond itself to an Other that bounds it, but is *with itself*. That being is **being-for-self** (*Fürsichsein*), and unfolding it is the work of this installment.

It is worth being precise about what kind of step this is. The whole movement from *Dasein* through Something, Limit, finitude, and the true infinite was driven by *the relation to an Other*. At every stage the determinate being was what it was by reference to something it was not. Being-for-self is the determination in which that long dependence is, for the first time, overcome — not by abolishing the Other but by *internalizing* it. The Other is still present, but only as a moment within, no longer as an outside. This is the qualitative sphere's last great determination; with it, and with the dialectic of the One and the Many that it generates, Quality completes itself and passes over into Quantity.

## II. Being-for-self and Ideality

Recall the two minimal sides of any determinacy, named long ago: **Reality** (*Realität*), the affirmative *being-thus*, and the negative side that, taken strictly, is the determinacy's *not-being-otherwise*. Through the whole career of the finite, the negative side appeared as a *relation to an Other outside*. In being-for-self that negative side is taken back inward: the negation is no longer the reference to an external other but the being's own sublatedness-of-otherness. This inward, sublated status of the otherwise-external is what Hegel calls **ideality** (*Idealität*).

Being-for-self is therefore the first category in which **ideality stands as such**. We met the *word* in the previous installment, in the proposition that the finite is *ideal*; but there it named the status the finite acquires *inside* the true infinite. Now we have the determination whose very content *is* that ideality: being that is affirmatively itself precisely because it has reduced its otherness to a moment. Reality and ideality are the qualitative pair that being-for-self holds together — the affirmative being-thus, and the sublated, internalized otherness that no longer stands against it. This is the consummation of the qualitative: a being that is fully determinate and yet, in its determinacy, *related only to itself*.

The temptation, here as everywhere, is to picture this as a *thing* that has swallowed its environment. Resist it. Being-for-self is not yet a thing, not an object, not a self in any psychological sense. It is the bare logical form of *self-relation that has its otherness within as ideal* — the form, as we will see, that the I, the atom, the monad, and the living individual will each later instantiate, but which at this level is only that form, stripped to its minimum.

## III. Being-for-one — the Other Turned Inward

How does the internalized otherness show itself within being-for-self? As the moment Hegel names, with deliberate awkwardness, **being-for-one** (*Sein-für-Eines*).

The phrase is built to be heard against its predecessor. *Being-for-other* (§12) was the side on which the Something stood exposed to an Other genuinely outside it. *Being-for-one* is what *being-for-other becomes* once the Other has been internalized: the being is now "for" something, yes — but the something it is for is no longer an external other; it is *itself*. Hegel makes the point through a quirk of German idiom. The ordinary question *"Was für ein Ding ist das?"* — "what kind of thing is this?" — says, word for word, "*what for a* thing is this?" In asking what something is *for*, ordinary speech already half-knows that to ask what a thing is *for* is to ask what it is *in itself*: the "for" and the "what it is" coincide. So in being-for-one, *that for which the being is, and the being itself, are one and the same*. The relation-to-other has become a relation-to-self conducted through an otherness that is now only ideal.

This is exactly the "transformed form" in which the Other survives that the previous installment promised. The Other is not gone — being-for-self would be empty without the otherness it has sublated — but it no longer stands over against the being as an independent term. It persists *as* the being's own being-for-one: as the ideal moment through which the being relates to itself. Hold this carefully, because the next move looks, at first, like a sudden hardening, and it can only be understood as the result of this internalization.

## IV. The One

Press being-for-self for what it now is. It is self-relation; its otherness is wholly internal and ideal; it refers, through that ideal otherness, only to itself. But a self-relation in which the otherness has become *entirely* ideal — utterly without standing of its own — is a self-relation with *no internal distinction left to articulate*. The two sides (the being, and that-for-which-it-is) have collapsed into bare identity. What remains is a simple, immediate, self-related unit that excludes all otherness from itself: the **One** (*das Eins*).

The One is the qualitative determination driven to its extreme of simplicity. It has three features, and they will generate everything that follows. First, it is **simple self-relation** — within it there is no manifold, no parts, no internal difference; it is the very form of indivisible unity. Second, it is **indeterminate** — but not as pure Being was indeterminate (through sheer emptiness); the One's indeterminateness is the *result* of a negation, the determinateness of having excluded all otherness, so it is indeterminate *by way of* being utterly self-enclosed. Third, and decisively, it is **self-relating negation**: the One *is* what it is only by negating, by excluding, by being-not-other. And this third feature is unstable in a precise way. A negation must negate *something*. The One has driven its otherness so wholly inside and made it so wholly ideal that it has, in effect, nothing left to negate — and yet *negating* is its very character. This is the pressure that will, in the next steps, force the One to posit an otherness over against itself once more: first as the void, then as other Ones.

## V. The One and the Void — Atomism and Its Logical Truth

The One is self-relating negation with nothing internal to negate. Its negativity must therefore appear *outside* it — as its own nothing, posited as distinct from it. That posited nothing, the negative set over against the One as the empty room in which the One is the only fullness, is the **Void** (*das Leere*).

The One and the Void: this is the logical skeleton of **atomism**, and Hegel pauses on it deliberately, for atomism is one of the great speculative achievements of ancient thought. Leucippus and Democritus grasped, with extraordinary penetration, that the structure of a world can be built from just two principles — the full (the atom, the One) and the empty (the void) — and the later atomists made the void the very source of motion and difference (so, at least, on Hegel's reading — historically Democritus treats the void rather as the room or condition for an eternal motion than as its source). Hegel's verdict is double, and the doubleness is instructive. On one side, he honors atomism as *speculative*: it seized the One and its negation as principles, which is a far deeper start than the sensuous philosophies of stuff that preceded it. On the other side, he diagnoses its one-sidedness: atomism *externalizes* the negativity. It treats the void as something simply *found*, lying outside the atom, when in truth the void is *the One's own negativity*, the One's own self-relating negation displaced outward and given a separate name. The atom and the void are not two independent finds; they are the One and its own immanent nothing, torn apart and set side by side.

The same diagnosis reaches forward to the modern physics of forces. When later physics replaces atoms-and-void with centers of force and the attractions and repulsions between them, it is working the *same* content at a higher level of articulation — and it inherits the *same* temptation, to treat the forces as independently subsisting things "had" by bodies, rather than as moments of a single self-relating negativity. We are not, here, deducing physics or correcting it; the *Logic* exhibits the logical form that any atomistic-pluralistic account of nature must take, and shows in advance where its one-sidedness lies. What that form *demands*, and what atomism-with-only-the-void cannot supply, is now to be drawn out: the One does not merely face an empty other; it posits *other Ones*.

## VI. Repulsion and Attraction

The One is self-relating negation. To relate to itself *as negation* is to repel: the One, in being the One it is, *repels itself from itself* and thereby posits **many Ones**. This positing of the Many by the One is **Repulsion** (*Repulsion*, *Abstoßung*). It is not that several Ones were lying about and pushed apart; it is that the very negativity constitutive of *one* One generates plurality. Plurality is not imported from outside the One; it is the One's own act.

But now watch the peculiar thing — the hinge on which the whole passage to Quantity turns. The many Ones that Repulsion posits are, each of them, *simply One*. Each is the same bare self-excluding unit; there is, within any of them, nothing to distinguish it from any other. The One repelled *itself*, and what it produced is only *itself over again*, indefinitely. So the plurality that Repulsion generates is a plurality of the *qualitatively indistinguishable*. The Ones differ *numerically* — there are many — but not *qualitatively* — each is just a One. Their distinction is therefore wholly external: it falls not into any One's character but only into the bare "and another, and another."

This self-sameness of the many Ones is, taken for what it is, their implicit *unity* — and that implicit unity, made explicit, is **Attraction** (*Attraktion*, *Anziehung*). Since each of the many Ones is the same One, their manyness is, in truth, null; they are *already* one and the same; and the positing of this oneness out of the dispersed Many is Attraction, the counter-movement that draws the Many back toward the One.

Repulsion and Attraction are not two independent forces that happen to coexist (the error of taking atomism's repulsion-and-void as complete, or of taking Newtonian attraction as a self-standing principle). **Each is only through the other.** Repulsion produces the Many that Attraction unifies; Attraction produces the One that Repulsion repels into Many. Neither can be had alone: a pure repulsion would scatter into a manyness whose members, being all the same, have already collapsed into unity (so repulsion has produced attraction); a pure attraction would draw everything into a One whose self-relating negation immediately repels again (so attraction has produced repulsion). They are two moments of a *single* movement — the self-relating negativity of the One, articulated as a process. The understanding holds them apart as two forces in balance; the truth is that they are one self-sublating activity.

## VII. The Self-Sublation of the Qualitative One — and the Passage into Quantity

Stand back and see what this single activity has done to the qualitative One. The One was supposed to be the determination of *exclusive*, *self-related* being — the very emblem of qualitative determinacy at its sharpest: *this* one, excluding all else. But the dialectic of Repulsion and Attraction has shown that the One's exclusiveness *undoes itself*. In repelling, it produces only copies of itself, so its "exclusion of the other" excludes nothing genuinely other; in attracting, it shows the many to be already the same, so its plurality is no real plurality. The qualitative determinacy of the One — its being *this* exclusive unit — has made itself **indifferent**. What is left is a manyness of units that are perfectly alike, externally related, each indifferent to the others and to its own place among them.

And *that* — a determinateness that has become indifferent to the being it determines, a plurality of self-same units whose only difference is the external "more or fewer" — is no longer Quality. It is **Quantity** (*Quantität*). In Quality, the determinacy *was* the being: to alter the quality was to make the thing cease to be *this* and become something else. In Quantity, the determinacy has come loose from the being: a thing can grow or shrink, be one or many, more or less, *without ceasing to be what it is*. The One-and-Many has produced exactly this loosening. The indifferent, repeatable, qualitatively-empty unit that the dialectic of Repulsion and Attraction leaves in its wake *is the unit of counting* — and with the unit of counting, the sphere of Quantity has opened.

This is the first passage from one sub-sphere of the Doctrine of Being to the next, and it has the form proper to the sphere of Being: it is a **passing-over** (*Übergehen*). Quality does not "shine into" Quantity (that relational mode belongs to Essence) nor "develop into" it (that belongs to the Concept); it *passes over* into it, by driving its own central determination — the exclusive One — to the point where exclusiveness becomes indifference and the qualitative determinant survives only as the bare, countable unit. Quality is not left behind; it is sublated. (Every quantum will still have, as we will see in the sphere of Quantity, a qualitative moment — a *this-much* that is, after all, a determinacy. The qualitative is preserved, in the thinned, being-sphere way the previous installment described. But the *governing* determination has changed: determinacy and being have come apart.)

## VIII. Three Misreadings, Answered Directly

As with "negation" and with "the infinite," three standing misreadings are worth meeting head-on.

The first is the **physicalist misreading**: that the One is an atom and the Void is empty space, so Hegel is doing armchair physics — deducing the structure of matter from his desk. This inverts the relation. The *Logic* does not deduce the physical atom; it exhibits the *logical form* — One-and-Void, Repulsion-and-Attraction — that any atomistic account of nature must take, whatever its empirical detail. Physics *instantiates* that form (and may instantiate it in ways no armchair could foresee: fields, quanta, forces of kinds Democritus never dreamt). What the *Logic* can say in advance is not *what* the physics will find but *that* a physics built on pure repulsion-and-void will prove one-sided and be driven to admit attraction — a structural prophecy that the history of physics has, in its own terms, borne out.

The second is the **reified-forces misreading**: that Repulsion and Attraction are two forces Hegel claims to derive, each a thing in its own right that bodies "have." This is precisely the error the section diagnoses. Repulsion and Attraction are not independent forces but *moments of one self-relating negativity*; to hypostatize either — to make repulsion-plus-void a complete account, or to erect attraction into a self-standing principle — is to repeat atomism's one-sidedness at the level of dynamics. Hegel's target here is not physics' use of forces but the *metaphysics* that takes forces to be ultimate, externally-related things.

The third is the **One-as-Absolute misreading**: that Hegel's One is the One of Parmenides or of Neoplatonism — the supreme Unity, the Absolute as One — or, at the other extreme, that it is already the arithmetical 1. Both are out of place. The numeral 1 belongs to Quantity, which has not yet opened; to read it back into *das Eins* is to skip the very transition this installment traces. And the One-as-Absolute belongs to a wholly different level of the system: the One here is the *minimal qualitative self-excluding unit*, the thinnest form of being-for-self, not the divine Unity of the whole. The whole interest of the passage is that *this* humble One, pressed, dissolves its own exclusiveness and gives birth not to the Absolute but to the countable unit.

## IX. What the Method Did Here — Observations

Two observations on the step, each holding at later steps as well.

The first: **a one-sidedness was made to summon its opposite, and the truth was their unity.** This is by now a familiar signature, but the present case sharpens it. Repulsion, taken alone, *produces* Attraction (its many turn out already one); Attraction, taken alone, *produces* Repulsion (its one immediately repels). Neither could be isolated even in thought; the attempt to hold one without the other collapses into the other. This is the same lesson as finitude-and-infinity, now in dynamical dress: the understanding's fixed, mutually-external determinations are, read for their own movement, moments of a single process. The reader who has internalized this no longer asks "which is fundamental, repulsion or attraction?" — the question itself belongs to the understanding. The fundamental thing is the self-relating negativity whose two faces they are.

The second, and deeper for the synopsis's governing theme: **here is where the unit of counting is born — and with it the ground of all quantification.** The "one" that mathematics and measurement presuppose — the bare, repeatable, qualitatively-empty unit that can be added to itself indifferently — is not a primitive given. It is a *logical result*: what the qualitative One becomes when its exclusiveness has worked itself into indifference. This matters greatly for the question that animates the whole synopsis — the place of Hegel's method in the scientific paradigm. Quantification, the very medium of exact science, is not the bedrock beneath which one cannot dig; it has a *ground*, and the ground is qualitative. Number begins where quality has made itself indifferent to its being. To see this is to see both why mathematics is so powerful (it works in the medium of pure indifferent determinacy, where everything is external and combinable) and why mathematics is not self-sufficient (its indifferent unit is the residue of a qualitative determination it has forgotten). The *Logic* will spend the whole sphere of Quantity, and then Measure, earning back the qualitative that the passage into Quantity set aside — and that long labor is the categorial truth behind the perennial scientific discovery that the merely quantitative, pursued far enough, turns qualitative again (at nodal points, thresholds, phase transitions, as Section II already signaled).

## X. What Comes Next

The next installment opens the second sub-sphere of the Doctrine of Being:

- **Quantity** (*Quantität*) — pure quantity as the sublated being-for-self, the determinacy that has become indifferent to its being; its two moments, **continuity** and **discreteness**, and why each requires the other; the **quantum** as limited quantity, and **number** (amount and unit) as its determinate form.
- **The quantitative infinite** — the endless progress of "ever larger / ever smaller," which we will recognize at once as a *spurious* infinite of the form already diagnosed, and the *qualitative* moment that lurks in the quantitative ratio.
- **The passage toward Measure** — how quantity, pursued to its limit, reveals that it is not after all indifferent to quality, and how the two rejoin in **Measure**, the truth of the sphere of Being and the threshold of Essence.

With the move into Quantity, the *Logic* has completed the sphere of **Quality** and begun the second of the three great determinations of Being.

---

## Coda

We have now watched the qualitative sphere close upon itself, and close in a way worth pausing over. It did not end by reaching some final, stable quality; it ended by a quality — the exclusive One — *driving its own determinacy into indifference*, so that the very sharpness of "this one, not that" wore itself smooth into "one more, and one more, all alike." There is something almost vertiginous in this: the most rigidly self-asserting of the qualitative categories, the One that excludes everything, is exactly the category that dissolves the qualitative into the indifference of the countable. The lesson is one the *Logic* will repeat in higher keys: a determination pressed to its extreme does not harden into permanence but tips over into its opposite. The exclusive One becomes the indifferent unit; quality becomes quantity. And the reader who has followed from pure Being to here has now seen the first complete sphere of the *Logic* trace its arc and pass beyond itself — not exhausted, but sublated, carried forward into a sphere where everything we have learned about determinacy will have to be learned again in the strange new element of the indifferent, the external, the more-and-less. *What was hardest to win — that determinacy is never merely fixed but always in movement — is exactly what makes the next sphere legible before we enter it.*
